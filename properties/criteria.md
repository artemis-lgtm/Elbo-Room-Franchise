# Site Selection Criteria: Elbo Room Heritage Licensed Locations

*Last verified: March 25, 2026*
*Status: DRAFT — Refine after first 2-3 pilot locations provide data*

---

## Core Principle

An Elbo Room licensed location is NOT a replica of the Fort Lauderdale flagship. It's a bar that embodies the same ethos: a neighborhood institution built to last. The building should feel like it belongs where it is, not like it was dropped there by a franchise.

**The question isn't "does this look like the Elbo Room?" It's "could this bar still be here in 50 years?"**

---

## Mandatory Requirements (Non-Negotiable)

### Location Characteristics
- [ ] **Walkable neighborhood** — must be in a pedestrian-friendly area, not a strip mall or highway location
- [ ] **Street-level with visibility** — corner locations preferred (Elbo Room DNA), but any high-visibility street-level works
- [ ] **Outdoor potential** — patio, sidewalk seating, or open-air element. The bar should connect to its street.
- [ ] **Not in a shopping center or food court** — heritage bars don't live in retail complexes
- [ ] **Within 1 mile of residential density** — this is a neighborhood bar, not a destination bar (tourist markets excepted for first wave)

### Market Characteristics
- [ ] **Population 100K+ in metro area** — enough foot traffic to sustain
- [ ] **Median household income $45K-$120K** — working class to upper-middle. Not luxury, not struggling.
- [ ] **Existing bar/restaurant scene** — the area needs to already have nightlife culture. We don't create scenes, we join them.
- [ ] **No more than 1 Heritage Licensed location per 500K metro population** — scarcity protects value

### Operator Requirements
- [ ] **Minimum 3 years bar/restaurant management experience** — we don't train novices
- [ ] **Local to the market** — the operator must know the neighborhood. No absentee licensees.
- [ ] **Financial capacity** — minimum $150K liquid assets for Heritage License, $300K for Heritage Partner
- [ ] **Alignment with heritage values** — operator must pass a values interview, not just a financial check

## Strong Preferences (Not Dealbreakers)

### Location Preferences
- **Buildings with history** — pre-1970s construction preferred. A Heritage License in a 2020 build feels wrong.
- **Corner locations** — echoes the Fort Lauderdale flagship, maximizes visibility
- **Existing bars available for conversion** — lower buildout cost, already zoned, existing customer base
- **Proximity to water** — coastal, lakefront, riverfront. Not required but strengthens the brand connection.
- **Historic districts** — areas where the community already values preservation

### Market Preferences
- **College towns** — built-in rotating customer base, bars become part of campus culture
- **Tourist markets** — novelty premium for first wave (Sable's Coyote Ugly insight applies here)
- **Cities with "preservation culture"** — markets where "the oldest bar in town" is a selling point, not trivia

## Target Market Tiers

### Tier 1: Florida First (Pilot Wave, 2027-2028)
Home state advantage. Austin knows the market. Licensing law is straightforward. Drive-distance from flagship for support.

**Priority markets:**
1. **Tampa / St. Petersburg** — strong bar scene, waterfront potential, growing metro
2. **Jacksonville Beach** — coastal, younger demographic, underserved for heritage concepts
3. **Key West** — tourist market with premium pricing, bars ARE the culture
4. **Delray Beach / Boca Raton** — upscale coastal, proximity to Fort Lauderdale

### Tier 2: Southeast Expansion (2029-2030)
Cultural proximity to the Elbo Room brand. Beach/waterfront markets where the story resonates.

**Priority markets:**
1. **Charleston, SC** — historic city, strong preservation culture, bar scene
2. **Savannah, GA** — open-container downtown, tourism + local, historic buildings available
3. **Nashville, TN** — bar culture capital, but saturated. Selective.
4. **New Orleans, LA** — heritage bar culture already exists. Pat O'Brien's territory. Proceed with caution.

### Tier 3: National Expansion (2031+)
Only after model is proven in Tier 1-2 markets.

**Priority markets:**
1. **Austin, TX** — bar culture, growth market, but rapidly changing (gentrification risk)
2. **San Diego, CA** — beach culture, strong bar scene, high income
3. **Portland, ME** — small but passionate bar culture, waterfront
4. **Asheville, NC** — craft beverage capital, preservation-minded

### Markets to AVOID
- **Las Vegas** — gimmick market, brand dilution risk
- **Times Square / tourist traps** — authenticity dies on contact
- **Suburban strip malls** — anywhere
- **Markets without existing bar culture** — we can't create demand for heritage bars where bars aren't valued

## Physical Space Guidelines

### Size
- **Minimum:** 1,200 sq ft (intimate bar, 80-100 capacity)
- **Ideal:** 2,000-3,500 sq ft (120-200 capacity)
- **Maximum:** 5,000 sq ft (Flagship tier only)

*Elbo Room Fort Lauderdale works because of its compact, high-energy feel. Licensed locations should avoid the "warehouse bar" trap where the space is too big to feel alive on a Tuesday.*

### Required Elements
- Full bar (obviously)
- Visible history wall — every location tells its own story AND the Elbo Room origin story
- Outdoor element (even small — a patio, a sidewalk table section)
- Jukebox or curated music system (no DJs as default — atmosphere is conversational, not performative)

### Prohibited Elements
- TVs covering every wall (2-3 max, for live sports only)
- Bottle service / VIP sections
- Dress codes
- Anything that says "nightclub" instead of "bar"

## Sable's Note (March 25, 2026)

> Each licensed Elbo Room should be its own bar with its own local identity, backed by the Elbo Room name and operational knowledge. Think of it less like "Elbo Room Tampa" and more like "this bar is an Elbo Room bar" — a seal of authenticity that says this place was built to last, not built to flip.

This principle drives site selection. We're not looking for blank spaces to stamp with a brand. We're looking for bars (existing or planned) that already have the DNA and want the credibility and system behind them.
