### Property Tax Impact
- Broward County millage rate: ~1.9%
- At $15M assessed: ~$285,000/year
- Total annual cost at $15M purchase: ~$1.5M (debt service + tax)

### SBA 504 Loan Structure
| Component | % | Amount (on $15M) | Rate | Terms |
|-----------|---|-------------------|------|-------|
| Bank Loan | 50% | $7.5M | Market (6-7%) | 25yr |
| SBA Debenture | 40% | $6M | Below market (fixed) | 20-25yr |
| Owner Equity | 10% | $1.5M | — | — |

**Equity requirement: $1.5M.** At conservative licensing pace, cumulative revenue hits $1.8M by 2030. Even after operating expenses for the licensing program (~40% of revenue), that's ~$1.08M in retained earnings. Combined with flagship cash flow growth, the $1.5M equity requirement is reachable by 2031.

### Gap Closing Timeline

| Path | Gap Closes By | Confidence |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Organic growth only (5%) | ~2036 | Low — no margin for error |
| Conservative licensing + growth | ~2032-2033 | Medium |
| Moderate licensing + growth | ~2030-2031 | Medium-High |
| Licensing + SBA 504 | ~2029-2030 | High (if SBA qualifies) |

**Licensing accelerates the timeline by 4-6 years.** That's the entire business case for the Heritage License Program — not the licensing revenue itself, but what it does to the property acquisition timeline.

## Key Assumptions & Risks

### Assumptions
1. 5% annual growth in flagship revenue (Austin's number)
2. Average licensed location grosses $1.2-$1.5M annually
3. Licensee churn rate: 5-8% per year after Year 3
4. SBA 504 qualification requires owner-occupied structure (entity structure TBD)
5. Property value doesn't spike above $15M before acquisition
6. Interest rates remain within 100bps of current levels

### Risk Factors
1. **Property value exceeds projections:** If Las Olas Ocean development drives land values higher, $15M may be understated by 2030+
2. **SBA disqualification:** Entity structure doesn't meet owner-occupied requirement
3. **Licensee failure:** Early location closures would damage brand before it's established
4. **Family alignment:** Multiple owners need to agree on capital allocation
5. **Competition:** Another heritage bar brand launches first (low probability but non-zero)
6. **Economic downturn:** Recession impacts both flagship and licensing pipeline

### What Breaks the Model
- Flagship revenue declines (destroys both borrowing capacity and licensing credibility)
- First 3 licensed locations underperform (kills pipeline momentum)
- Property owner sells to developer before 2036 (accelerates timeline catastrophically)
- Family disagrees on expansion (stops everything)
