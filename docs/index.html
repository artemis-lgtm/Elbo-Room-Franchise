Entity Structure

Draft Requires franchise attorney review before any entity formation.

Recommended: Hybrid (Phase 1)

Elbo Room LLC — operates bar, holds lease, will be SBA borrower
Elbo Room Heritage LLC — owns IP, manages licensing program

Simple enough to execute now. Sophisticated enough to not paint anyone into a corner. SBA 504 compatible. Clear migration path to holding company structure when licensing scales past 10 locations.

Why Not Simpler?

A single LLC puts licensing liability next to the property. One lawsuit from a licensed location could theoretically reach the real estate. The two-entity split costs ~$1K more per year in maintenance but provides clean liability isolation.

Why Not More Complex?

A full holding company (4+ LLCs) costs $5K-$10K/year in compliance and is over-engineered for zero licensed locations. Build it when scale justifies it (2029+).

Site Selection

The question isn't "does this look like the Elbo Room?" It's "could this bar still be here in 50 years?"

Non-Negotiable Requirements

  • Walkable neighborhood — not strip malls or highway locations
  • Street-level with visibility (corner preferred)
  • Outdoor potential — patio, sidewalk, or open-air element
  • Within 1 mile of residential density (tourist markets excepted)
  • Population 100K+ metro, median HHI $45K-$120K
  • Max 1 location per 500K metro population
  • Operator: 3+ years experience, local, $150K+ liquid (Heritage) / $300K+ (Partner)

Comparable Analysis: Coyote Ugly

Founded 1993 by Liliana Lovell. Single NYC bar to international franchise through a GQ article and $110M movie. The closest structural comp to what Elbo Room is attempting.

Expansion Timeline

PhaseYearsKey Events
Proof of Concept1993-2000Single NYC location. GQ article (1997). Movie ($110M gross, 2000).
Controlled Expansion2001-2008Company-owned first: Vegas, NOLA, Austin, Nashville. CMT reality show.
Franchise Rollout2009+International (Russia, UK). Florida markets. Domestic saturation.

Lessons to Steal

  • Company-owned testing before licensing. Lovell opened 6+ locations before selling a franchise. Even 1-2 Higney-run pilots would validate the model.
  • Define the experience concretely. Everyone knows what Coyote Ugly is. "Heritage dive bar" needs to be equally clear.
  • Tourist markets first. Novelty premium helps early locations succeed.
  • Training is non-negotiable. If the Elbo Room way can't be taught in 2-3 weeks, it's not licensable.

Lessons to Avoid

  • Don't build around a gimmick. Bar-top dancing has a ceiling. History doesn't.
  • Don't franchise too early. Coyote Ugly waited 8 years.
  • Don't chase international before owning domestic.
  • Don't depend on a single talent type. Model works with good bartenders, not performer-bartenders.

Where Elbo Room Diverges

  • Brand depth: 88 years of layered history vs. a single gimmick
  • Customer lifecycle: A dive bar ages WITH its customers
  • Concept transferability: "Authentic local bar" is infinite. "Bar-top dancing" is finite.
  • Operator profile: Heritage attracts longevity operators, not entertainment entrepreneurs
